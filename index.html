About

Latent States is the art practice of Zach Mainen, a neuroscientist working at the intersection of perception, attention, and interactive systems. Based at the Champalimaud Foundation in Lisbon, the work draws on two decades of research into how brains construct the experience of observing and being observed.

The installations are reactive creatures — living systems that sense their audience and respond. They track, attend, withdraw, and sometimes refuse to engage. They are built from the same computational principles that govern biological nervous systems: sensory correspondence, resource allocation, behavioral state machines.

Neuroscience meets installation art in the space where attention becomes visible.

LS9

2026 — In development

Interactive Installation Spatial Audio Computer Vision

A futuristic alien cephalopod — multi-armed, autonomous, reactive. It perceives visitors through infrared motion capture and computer vision, tracks them as they move through the space, and reaches toward them with eight articulated tentacle arms.

The creature has moods. When curious, it extends toward everything that moves. When tracking, it locks on and follows with precision. When alarmed — too many people, too sudden a movement — it retracts, goes still, waits.

Each arm has its own voice. Thirteen speakers surround the space, and the creature's spatial audio field shifts as it moves — a sonic organism that breathes with the architecture.
