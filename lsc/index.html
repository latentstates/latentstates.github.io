Latent States Camp

A laboratory without walls. Since 2023, Latent States Camp has taken neuroscience out of the clinic and into a festival — designing experiments on perception, consciousness, and collective experience in the place where experience actually happens.

The Idea

What if you studied perception where perception is most alive? Not in a windowless lab with undergraduate subjects, but at a festival — outdoors, among strangers, under open sky, with all the noise and beauty and altered states that come with it. Latent States Camp is built on the conviction that the most interesting questions about experience can only be asked where experience is richest.

2023 — The Pilot

First year. Arrived at Waking Life as a creative camp. Phenomenological interviews — deep, unstructured conversations with festival-goers about their experience. Testing whether rigorous science could happen in the wild.

2024 — Seven Experiments

The expansion. 300+ participants, four main experiments running daily:

  • Seeing — eye-tracking under psychedelics. Finding: reduced eye movement links to stronger visual effects.
  • Hearing — VR auditory hallucination detection task (with Ott lab)
  • Tasting — cross-modal flavour perception with colored vessels
  • Shared Experience — phenomenological interviews continued

Plus Submersion (interactive AI-art, nightly) and time perception studies.

[Img. 001] Seeing experiment — eye-tracking at Waking Life 2024

2025 — The Outpost

Refined and focused. Professional camp structure. Core experiments: Seeing (three real-world scenarios), Hearing (continuation), Tasting (flavour naming), Submersion (nightly sound-reactive installation). New: Festival Effect longitudinal study measuring wellbeing across the festival. Kosmicare harm reduction partnership.

Its aim is to bring together scientists, researchers, artists and festival goers, to explore the diversity and complexity of the wilds of the human mind.
