Zachary Mainen · Champalimaud Foundation
Latent States is the art-science practice of Zachary Mainen, a neuroscientist at the Champalimaud Foundation in Lisbon.
The practice began in 2016 at Boom festival with Psiloscope — a hundred sensors on a dance floor — and has since grown into a body of work spanning interactive installations, immersive AI experiences, a festival research programme, and an autonomous creature that perceives its visitors.
The core conviction: certain questions about experience — what it is like to perceive oneself, the world, other people, a machine — cannot be answered in the laboratory, because the laboratory eliminates the conditions that produce the phenomena. Art creates the disposition to explore: curiosity without agenda, attention without obligation, presence without optimization. Festivals create contexts where perception operates under conditions the lab cannot replicate.
The theoretical framework underneath this practice is the Topology of Things Theory (TTT), which argues that reality is relational, continuous, and exceeds any single formal description. Changing perception doesn’t distort reality — it reveals worlds that were already there. The diversity of latent spaces is the diversity of worlds.
Latent States is part of the Champalimaud Foundation’s Bridges to the Unknown programme.
