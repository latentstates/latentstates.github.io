Metamersion: Latent Passages
2022–2023
A multimedia art piece combining film, installation, and VR — where artists, neuroscientists, and AI collaborate to imagine what future therapy could look like. You sit at a vintage terminal and have a conversation with an AI simulating the philosopher Alan Watts. Large language models analyze your responses. Then you enter VR — a dream-like visual journey shaped by what you said, rendered through latent blending with generated narration and custom music. The question at the center: what happens when a machine builds a world from your words?

The Installation
Metamersion 2 at the Champalimaud Foundation warehouse: the multi-pod physical space where visitors entered conversations with AI, and the large multi-screen displays showing real-time generated visual experiences. Each participant received a unique dream sequence rendered from their interaction.
The Work
Sixteen key frames from the film and VR experience. The short film was outlined using GPT-3 and post-produced with Stable Diffusion. An AI model trained on images of the lead talent generated alternate versions of the character — each screening a different dream.
AI Exploration
During post-production, a Stable Diffusion model was trained on images of lead talent Siori Lee. The model generated hundreds of variations — surrealist, cyberpunk, photorealistic — each a different version of the character as imagined by the machine. These explorations informed the final film and enabled twenty unique versions of the piece.
Behind the Scenes
The film was shot on location in Germany and Portugal. The installation was built at the Champalimaud Foundation warehouse in Lisbon for Metamersion 2, December 2022 – January 2023.

“We were trying to imagine how AI would create this environment for people, to feel somehow viscerally connected and have these experiences that maybe help them grow. And at some point, we said, but why don’t we just ask AI? Why don’t we just use these models that are already available on the web and just try to see how it would create itself?”

Octavian Mot, film director, mots

“We wanted to get away from the very restricted task and try to study people in more natural environments. These language and image generation models have this creativity, they’re not reliable, and they tend to do unexpected things. We were seeing this as an amazing advantage — they provide a much more naturalistic context for looking at people interacting with something that is not a thousand times exactly the same boring thing.”

Zachary Mainen, neuroscientist, Champalimaud Centre for the Unknown

The Piece
Two components: a 3-minute short film written and post-produced with AI, and a virtual reality experience tailored to each participant. In the VR component, visitors conversed with a chatbot analyzed by language models. Based on that interpretation, a unique 3-minute video story was generated on the spot — visuals, sound, and narration made entirely by AI.
